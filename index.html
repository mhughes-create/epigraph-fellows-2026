Tech Turning Point
Learning Premiere changed how I direct. I was at BuzzFeed, expected to edit, and no one actually taught me like they said they would‚ so I taught myself. It felt like learning a new language while being expected to already be fluent. I stayed late, came in early, and worked weekends unpaid trying to figure it out. It was really difficult. Once it clicked, it changed everything. Editing taught me rhythm‚ when something drags, when it needs to snap forward, and how performance is shaped in the cut.
AI Concern
I love 2D animation, and what concerns me is how quickly people treat it as something that can be replicated with a prompt. There's a difference between generating an image and building a visual language over time with intention and collaboration. Animation is inherently collaborative‚ every frame is shaped by multiple artists‚ and I never want that to go away. My concern isn't the tools, but the idea that speed could replace craft.

Work Sample
In New York, I return to the birthplace of Hip Hop to explore a deeper truth‚ this culture was born from the shared experience of Black and Latino communities. The story was first told as a 90-minute feature that premiered at the Tribeca Film Festival before being condensed into a one-hour television format.
AI Comfort Level
● Intermediate — use some tools regularly
Tools Used
ChatGPT / Claude (writing/development) Midjourney / DALLE (visual ideation) Adobe Firefly / Photoshop AI
Tech Turning Point
Launching Hard Knock TV on YouTube in 2006 was a turning point for me. It allowed me to bypass traditional gatekeepers and tell the kind of stories I wasn't seeing elsewhere, directly to an audience that was already there. As a multi-hyphenate, I've always had to be resourceful without access to big budgets. Technology allows me to move at the speed of culture, execute at a higher level, and maintain creative control and cultural authenticity.
AI Concern
One concern I have is that as these tools scale, they risk diluting perspective‚ where stories begin to feel generated rather than lived. There's also the risk of unequal access, where certain communities are left behind, and built-in biases that shape which stories are seen. As a filmmaker, I'm focused on making sure technology amplifies cultural specificity and lived experience, rather than replacing it.

Tech Turning Point
Visual reference tools like ShotDeck helped shape tone and visual language during development. On projects like Peripheral, creating custom LUTs and baking them into dailies allowed the footage to reflect the intended look from the moment we began shooting.
AI Concern
My main concern is over dependence. I see AI the same way I see CGI or VFX as a supplementary tool, not a foundational one. It should enhance strong ideas and storytelling, not replace the core craft and fundamentals of filmmaking.
